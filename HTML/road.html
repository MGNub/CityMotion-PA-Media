ROADS

The purpose of roads is to transport people and goods fast and efficiently. Roads are high-speed thoroughfares, which are connections between 2 places.

STREETS

While roads serve as routes or pathways between destinations with a greater distance, streets serve as local routes for vehicles and pedestrians.

SUMMARY

Facilitates Movement

Roads and streets provide pathways for vehicles, pedestrians, cyclists, and other modes of transportation to move from one location to another. Well-maintained road infrastructure ensures timely access to critical services during emergencies.

Safety & Accessibility

Well-designed roads and streets prioritize safety for all users, including pedestrians, cyclists, motorists, and public transportation passengers.

Urban Development

Street designs influence development of neighborhoods, commercial districts, and public spaces. Roads connecting cities and regions promote economic growth, trade, and tourism, contributing to regional growth and prosperity.

STROADS

While streets and roads together make up a coherent infrastructure, here's where stroads come into the mix.

What is a Stroad?

Overall, stroads are wide multi-laned thoroughfares with residential or commercial buildings alongside it.

Stroads have a lot more exits and entrances than a typical highway, making the traffic both slower and more dangerous.

Overall, a stroad is a hybrid of a street and a road. They are expensive to build and maintain, they slow down traffic for drivers, and are hostile towards pedestrians. The problem is that they're harmful to the city, they're harmful to the people, and they're everywhere.

Highways

Not only are stroads dangerous for pedestrians, but they are also inconvenient for drivers as well, because of the slow traffic. The same thing can also be said about highways in America. While not all highways face the same issues, there are some highways with much higher traffic congestion than others, such as I-10 in Texas or I-290 in Illinois.

Solution?

More lanes are usually added to highways in order to reduce traffic congestion. However, this is only a temporary fix. Once there's less traffic congestion, it'll encourage more people to use the highway, which will end up increasing traffic congestion to the same state as before. This is called induced traffic. It becomes a cycle of building more lanes, inducing more traffic, before building more lanes again. This is basically what happened to the I-10 in Houston, Texas, which boats a maximum of 26 lanes.

Not only is expanding these highways expensive, they are only a short-term solution to traffic congestion, and they also come at the cost of using up land. Oftentimes, many parks, houses, and even whole neighborhoods are bulldozed to make room for these highway expansions.

The real solution is to reduce the number of cars on the road, by making other methods of transportation more reliable. This will also allow better and more efficient use of land, which will improve the quality life within the city

Conclusion

There are a bunch of smaller things that can be done to improve the safety of roads and make them more accessible to everyone. For example, adding speed humps in needed areas, building more roundabouts, narrowing lanes, and dedicating spaces for pedestrians.

Improving public transportation or biking infrastructure will also improve experiences for both drivers and non-drivers. The next page will be diving deeper into the correlation between good public transit and the traffic on roads.
