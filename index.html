Reinventing Cities

Today, the country of America suffers from heavy carcentrism, leading to poor city infrastructure. The cities of America are designed for the car, built around the car, and bulldozed for the car.

The Beginning

With the end of World War 2 came a huge change in America’s infrastructure. There was a heavy focus on suburbanization, which led to problems with traffic and road safety. The passenger rail system was made redundant due to the expansions of interstate highways and airlines, and the use of automobiles has seen an explosive growth.

Cities today have many issues regarding the efficiency of travel and the safety of pedestrians and drivers alike. For the future economic growth and development of America, a change is needed in the current infrastructure. This can only happen if citizens group together and opt for change.
